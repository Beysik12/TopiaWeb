Saving the world one algorithm at a time: The Age of AI

The idea of algorithmic bias is intriguing: it occurs when a computer system behaves in ways that are reflective of the beliefs or preferences of the people who worked on its design or data collection. In essence, it's similar to reading a story in a newspaper that expresses the viewpoints and political leanings of the person who wrote it. We are extremely familiar with this idea, but while the majority of us can apply critical thinking to an article's substance and read between the lines, it is much harder to do the same with an algorithm. As a result, we assign this task to the faith we have in an organization. In fact, a programmer would create an algorithm in a typical system, which would be given data, and you would receive a response. In the future, a computer would be fed a ton of data and the results that corresponded with it, and it would create an algorithm. a logic-based algorithm that is alien. The question of how to ensure that this new army of AI-based decision makers is and remains ethical arises since humankind should be concerned with improving the planet. AI is not a new scourge; rather, it is essential for improving humans and leading more satisfying lives.
